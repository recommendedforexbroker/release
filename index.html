Why Execution Model Matters: A-Book vs B-Book Brokers

Many UK traders don't realise that some brokers profit when you lose. These are B-Book brokers — they take the opposite side of your trade. This can lead to conflicts of interest, order manipulation, and trust issues.

A-Book brokers, on the other hand, simply act as intermediaries. Your trades are passed directly to liquidity providers — typically large financial institutions.

This model offers several key benefits:

  • Transparent trade execution (no dealing desk intervention)
  • Fair, market-based pricing with no manipulation or requotes
  • No conflict of interest between trader and broker

In other words, your success is not your broker's loss — a crucial consideration when choosing a platform.

Regulation and Trustworthiness in the UK Market

For UK traders, regulation is more than a checkbox — it's a safeguard. You want a broker that is authorised and regulated by Tier-1 authorities, or at least operates under strict international frameworks. These can be:

  • FCA
  • ASIC (Australia)
  • CySEC (Europe)
